Webhooks look simple on the surface and quickly become hard once you ship a few. The five pages in this section cover the concepts every Postel integration eventually touches.

- **[Signing and verification](/docs/concepts/signing-and-verification)** — what the receiver actually checks, the v1 (HMAC) and v1a (Ed25519) signature schemes, and why constant-time comparison matters.
- **[Key rotation](/docs/concepts/key-rotation)** — the multi-secret window, JWKS publication, and how to rotate a leaked secret without a flag day.
- **[Idempotency](/docs/concepts/idempotency)** — webhooks are at-least-once. How the dedup helper makes them once-effectively, atomically.
- **[Raw bytes](/docs/concepts/raw-bytes)** — the most common silent failure mode in webhook receivers. Why you must never `JSON.stringify(JSON.parse(body))`.
- **[Edge runtimes](/docs/concepts/edge-runtimes)** — what the 50 KB bundle budget and Web-Crypto-only constraint buy you, and what they cost.

If you're new, [signing and verification](/docs/concepts/signing-and-verification) is the right starting page.
